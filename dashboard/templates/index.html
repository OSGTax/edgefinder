How EdgeFinder Works

EdgeFinder is a self-improving paper trading system that combines fundamental analysis, technical signals, and news sentiment filtering to simulate intelligent stock trading. It starts rule-based and graduates to ML as data accumulates.

The Pipeline

Every trade decision flows through a multi-layer pipeline. Each layer acts as a filter, ensuring only high-conviction setups reach execution.

1 Fundamental Scanner
Runs nightly. Screens ~8,000 stocks using Peter Lynch (growth at a reasonable price) and Michael Burry (deep value, balance sheet strength) criteria. Produces a watchlist of ~50-100 candidates scored 0-100. Lynch weight: 50%, Burry weight: 50%.
2 Technical Signal Engine
Monitors watchlist stocks every 15-30 minutes during market hours. Looks for entry signals using RSI, MACD, EMA crossovers, and volume confirmation. Generates BUY/SELL signals with a confidence score for both day and swing trade setups.
2.5 News Sentiment Gate
Go/no-go filter before any trade executes. Uses VADER sentiment analysis on recent news headlines. Blocks trades with strongly negative sentiment to avoid catching falling knives.
3 Paper Trader
Simulated execution with full risk management. Enforces position sizing (2% max risk per trade), PDT limits (3 day trades per 5 days), stop losses, trailing stops, and daily/weekly loss limits. Starting capital: $2,500.
4 Trade Journal
Logs every trade and every skipped signal with full context — why it entered, why it exited, what indicators fired, and what the sentiment was. This data feeds the optimizer.
5 Strategy Optimizer
Weekly analysis of trade outcomes. Adjusts parameters (thresholds, weights, timing) based on what’s working and what isn’t. Requires 50+ logged trades to be meaningful.

Scheduler

The system runs automatically during market hours (9:30 AM – 4:00 PM ET, Mon-Fri). The scanner runs at 6:00 AM ET before market open. Signal checking runs every 15 minutes during trading hours. Account snapshots are taken at market close.

What You're Seeing

Watchlist — Stocks that passed fundamental screening, ranked by composite score.
Signals — Technical entry/exit signals detected on watchlist stocks.
Trades — Simulated trades that were executed, with P&L tracking.
Skipped — Signals that fired but weren’t traded (and why).
Suggestions — Submit ideas for new features, strategy changes, or bug reports.

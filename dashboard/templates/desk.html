Trading Desk

Live prices
Real-time prices straight from the exchanges — the same numbers the AI trades on. "Bid" is what buyers offer, "ask" is what sellers want.
Options radar
What the options market expects for a stock — how big a move traders are bracing for, and what protection costs. Type any symbol. Hover any term for a plain-English explanation.
Market movers
The biggest winners, losers, and most-traded names across the ~500 most-liquid stocks the AI tracks — as of the last market close. A read on where the action is, not the AI's own picks.
Account value over time
The whole account (cash + investments), recorded every trading cycle. Started at $100,000.
What the AI owns right now
Every investment currently held, what it cost, what it's worth now, and the gain or loss so far.
The AI thinking out loud
Its live reasoning, in its own words, as each trading cycle runs.
Dividend calendar
For each holding that pays a dividend: the most recent payment, the next one on the calendar, and the estimated yearly payout per share. A dividend is a cash payment a company sends shareholders.
The latest decision
What the AI decided on its most recent cycle — what it bought, held, or sold, and the reasoning behind each pick.
Recent fills
Every trade the AI made, with the exact live bid and ask it priced against at that moment — the receipt proving each fill hit the real market.
What the AI has learned
A small notebook the AI keeps of lessons from its own wins and losses — rewritten as real results come in, never from hunches. Lightly reviewed every cycle; deeply reviewed every Friday after the close. Every edit is logged in the Strategy diary.
Homework: history tests
Before betting (paper) money on an idea, the AI tests how it would have performed in past markets versus simply buying the S&P 500.
Strategy diary
Every time the AI changes its approach, it writes down what changed and why.
